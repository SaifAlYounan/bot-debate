ID: LENS_A-1
SIDE: FOR
CLAIM: Priced at loaded hourly cost, the marginal recurring meeting destroys value.
REASONING: Multiply attendance by cost and the negative tail is visible; a ban prunes it cheaply.
DEFEATER: Portfolio data showing the Friday mix is not disproportionately low-value.

ID: LENS_A-2
SIDE: AGAINST
CLAIM: A price mechanism dominates a quantity ban.
REASONING: Internal meeting budgets let high-value meetings continue and kill low-value ones; bans cannot discriminate on value.
DEFEATER: Transaction costs of the budget scheme exceeding its allocative gains.

ID: LENS_A-3
SIDE: FOR
CLAIM: Focus time is a public good that individual scheduling under-provides.
REASONING: Any one person accepting a meeting imposes fragmentation on shared work; a collective rule solves the assurance problem.
DEFEATER: Evidence that informal norms already protect focus blocks.

ID: LENS_A-4
SIDE: AGAINST
CLAIM: A uniform office rule guarantees allocative error across heterogeneous teams.
REASONING: Efficient meeting quantity varies by an order of magnitude across functions.
DEFEATER: Evidence that within-office variance in efficient meeting load is small.

ID: LENS_A-5
SIDE: REFRAME
CLAIM: Measured substitution elasticity should decide the question.
REASONING: If demand mostly shifts to Thursday the welfare gain is near zero; the debate should centre on that number.
DEFEATER: A natural experiment measuring substitution near zero.

ID: LENS_A-6
SIDE: AGAINST
CLAIM: Compliance and monitoring costs recur forever.
REASONING: Every exception, audit and dispute is labour; steady-state administration routinely exceeds projections.
DEFEATER: Fully automated calendar enforcement approaching zero recurring cost.

ID: LENS_A-7
SIDE: FOR
CLAIM: A specialist in labour economics would add: target measured output, not meeting hours.
REASONING: Meeting hours are an input proxy weakly correlated with productivity.
DEFEATER: If team output is unmeasurable here, input proxies are all that is actionable.

ID: LENS_A-8
SIDE: REFRAME
CLAIM: The debate needs a stated discount rate for culture-change payoffs.
REASONING: Ban benefits arrive immediately; adaptations and costs arrive over quarters, so the sides talk past each other without a time preference.
DEFEATER: Demonstration that both streams share a time profile.
