ID: M-1
SIDE: FOR
CLAIM: A protected meeting-free day preserves deep work by removing calendar fragmentation.
REASONING: Long uninterrupted stretches are the binding input for knowledge work; a fixed quiet day changes the weekly equilibrium and compounds as planning adapts.
DEFEATER: Evidence that fragmented calendars do not reduce measured output for most roles.
