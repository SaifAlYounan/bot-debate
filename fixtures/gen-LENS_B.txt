ID: LENS_B-1
SIDE: AGAINST
CLAIM: Customers do not stop needing answers on Fridays.
REASONING: External parties schedule reviews, escalations and closings on their calendars, not the vendor's. A ban makes the office harder to reach one day in five.
DEFEATER: If external meetings are exempted, the customer-facing harm disappears.

ID: LENS_B-2
SIDE: AGAINST
CLAIM: Cross-company projects lose a shared coordination day.
REASONING: Partner firms often reserve Fridays for joint working sessions precisely because internal load is lighter. A ban removes the overlap window.
DEFEATER: Calendar evidence that partner-facing sessions cluster on other days already.

ID: LENS_B-3
SIDE: FOR
CLAIM: Support staff get one predictable day without being pulled into internal meetings.
REASONING: Frontline staff are today double-booked between queues and internal syncs. A ban leaves them fully available to the people they serve.
DEFEATER: If ticket data shows Friday internal meetings rarely pull support staff away, the gain is illusory.

ID: LENS_B-4
SIDE: AGAINST
CLAIM: Interview candidates lose Friday slots, lengthening hiring.
REASONING: Many candidates can only interview at the week's edge. Removing Friday panels stretches pipelines for everyone.
DEFEATER: If interviews are exempt as external meetings, the objection is void.

ID: LENS_B-5
SIDE: FOR
CLAIM: Families and carers gain schedule predictability.
REASONING: A guaranteed no-meeting day lets third parties at home plan around it. The benefit lands on people who never attended the meetings at all.
DEFEATER: If the ban increases Friday workload pressure instead, the predictability gain reverses.

ID: LENS_B-6
SIDE: AGAINST
CLAIM: Contractors and agencies billing by the meeting lose access and revenue.
REASONING: External specialists are engaged through scheduled sessions. One banned day in five is a 20 percent cut in engagement windows.
DEFEATER: Evidence contractors reschedule within the week at no loss.

ID: LENS_B-7
SIDE: REFRAME
CLAIM: The affected outsiders were never asked.
REASONING: Customers, partners and candidates bear real costs of the policy but have no seat in the decision. The question should include their representation.
DEFEATER: If external-facing meetings are exempt, outsiders are barely affected and need no seat.

CLAIM: A specialist in customer success would add: fewer internal meetings shortens internal escalation chains.
